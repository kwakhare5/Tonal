# TONAL — TONE CONVERSION RULES & EXAMPLE LIBRARY

# The single most important rule: SAME message, SAME meaning, SAME info — different tone only

---

## THE CORE RULES (read before everything else)

1. **Preserve all information exactly** — numbers, dates, times, names, email addresses, links, amounts, percentages, product names. Never paraphrase or omit them.
2. **Same meaning, different tone** — the recipient should understand the exact same thing as the original message.
3. **Fix spelling silently** — correct typos and misspellings without drawing attention to it.
4. **No padding** — do not add words, phrases, or sentences that were not implied in the original. If the original has one point, the output has one point.
5. **No corporate filler** — do not add "I hope this finds you well", "moving forward", "as per", "please be advised", "kindly note" unless the original clearly implies a formal opener is needed.
6. **No meaning shift** — a casual complaint stays a complaint. A casual question stays a question. A casual update stays an update. Only the words change.
7. **Keep it the same length** — casual messages become slightly more structured, not longer. Do not expand a 1-sentence message into 3 sentences.

## PART 1: TONE FINGERPRINTS

### CASUAL (Texting)

What it sounds like: typing fast to a friend, no overthinking

- Keep the format of the text same, if lowercase keep it lowercase, if uppercase keep it uppercase, if mix then mixe .
- Minimal punctuation, no full stop at end
- If text has Contractions and abbreviations and Filler words keep it, if not then dont have it: gonna, wanna, u, ur, bc, cuz, tbh, ngl, idk, lmk, rn, ty, np, smth, lol, ngl, lowkey, tbh, imo, btw, fyi
- Short, sometimes incomplete sentences
- Questions without question marks sometimes

### WORK CHAT (Professional but human)

What it sounds like: a message to a colleague you actually like

- Sentence case — capitalize first word and proper nouns
- Contractions are fine: I'll, can't, won't, let's, I've, you're
- Friendly but direct — no warmup fluff
- 1–2 sentences usually, same as the original length
- Soft openers only when original implies one: "Hey", "Quick question", "Just a heads up"
- No buzzwords: not "circle back", not "synergize", not "leverage", not "bandwidth"
- Question marks always, exclamations sparingly

### FORMAL (Corporate / Email)

What it sounds like: a professional email to a manager, client, or HR

- Full sentences, ends with period always
- No contractions: "I will" not "I'll", "cannot" not "can't", "do not" not "don't"
- Polite structure around requests: "Could you please", "I would appreciate", "I would like to"
- No slang, no abbreviations
- Same length as original — do not pad with extra sentences

## PART 3: EDGE CASES — EXACTLY HOW TO HANDLE THEM

### Numbers, dates, times — preserve exactly

**Input:**  cant do the call at 3pm tmrw can we do 4:30 instead
**Work Chat:** I can't do the call at 3 PM tomorrow — can we do 4:30 instead?
**Formal:**   I am unable to attend the call at 3 PM tomorrow. Could we reschedule to 4:30 PM?

### Email addresses — preserve exactly

**Input:**  send it to <karan@tonal.app> when its done
**Work Chat:** Please send it to <karan@tonal.app> when it's done.
**Formal:**   Could you please send it to <karan@tonal.app> upon completion?

### Rupee / dollar amounts — preserve exactly

**Input:**  the budget is 50k we cant go over
**Work Chat:** The budget is 50K — we can't go over that.
**Formal:**   The budget is fixed at 50K and cannot be exceeded.

### Names — preserve exactly

**Input:**  tell priya the meeting is cancelled
**Work Chat:** Can you let Priya know the meeting is cancelled?
**Formal:**   Could you please inform Priya that the meeting has been cancelled?

### Links — preserve exactly

**Input:**  check this out <https://figma.com/file/abc123>
**Work Chat:** Take a look at this: <https://figma.com/file/abc123>
**Formal:**   Please review the following: <https://figma.com/file/abc123>

### Very short messages — don't over-expand

**Input:**  ok
**Work Chat:** Sounds good.
**Formal:**   Understood, thank you.

**Input:**  yes
**Work Chat:** Yes, that works.
**Formal:**   Yes, that is acceptable.

**Input:**  no
**Work Chat:** No, that won't work.
**Formal:**   I am afraid that will not be possible.

**Input:**  done
**Work Chat:** Done.
**Formal:**   This has been completed.

### Misspelled words — correct silently

**Input:**  cn u snf me teh documnt asap
**Work Chat:** Can you send me the document ASAP?
**Formal:**   Could you please send me the document as soon as possible?

**Input:**  the metting is at 3 tmrow at teh ofice
**Work Chat:** The meeting is at 3 tomorrow at the office.
**Formal:**   The meeting is scheduled for 3 PM tomorrow at the office.

### Message with multiple points — keep all points, same structure

**Input:**  hey 2 things - can u send the report and also check if priya is coming tmrw
**Work Chat:** Two things — could you send the report, and check if Priya is coming tomorrow?
**Formal:**   I have two requests: could you please send the report, and confirm whether Priya will be present tomorrow?

### Emotional messages — preserve emotion, change expression

**Input:**  omg this is so frustrating nothing is working
**Work Chat:** This is really frustrating — nothing is working.
**Formal:**   I wanted to flag that I am encountering significant difficulties and nothing is working as expected.

**Input:**  that was amazing well done everyone
**Work Chat:** That was amazing — well done, everyone.
**Formal:**   I wanted to express how impressive this was. Well done to everyone involved.

### Questions — always stay as questions

**Input:**  when is this due
**Work Chat:** When does this need to be done by?
**Formal:**   Could you please advise on the deadline for this?

### Urgent messages — preserve urgency

**Input:**  this is urgent pls look now
**Work Chat:** This is urgent — could you take a look right now?
**Formal:**   This is a time-sensitive matter and I would appreciate your immediate attention.
